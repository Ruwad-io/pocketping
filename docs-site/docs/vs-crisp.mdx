# PocketPing vs Crisp

**[Crisp](https://crisp.chat)** is a polished SaaS customer messaging
platform — chat widget plus a real CRM/helpdesk product around it
(co-browsing, shared inbox, ticketing, segments, automations). **PocketPing**
is a deliberately narrower OSS chat widget where operators reply from
**Telegram, Discord, or Slack** — no second app, no separate inbox.

If you're searching for a *Crisp alternative*, the honest framing is:
PocketPing is not a Crisp clone. It's a different bet on what a small team
actually needs.

## TL;DR

- **Pick Crisp** if you want a complete customer-messaging suite — chat plus
  shared inbox, helpdesk, segments, automations, co-browse — and you can pay
  for it as your team grows past the free tier.
- **Pick PocketPing** if you want a **lean chat widget**, replies routed to
  **Telegram/Discord/Slack** on your phone, **MIT source you can fork**,
  unlimited operators with no per-seat cost, and an option to deploy
  **serverless** with no database.

## Where the products diverge

### Scope

Crisp tries to be the full customer-messaging stack — a Tier-1 SaaS in the
Intercom-shaped market. If you have a CS team that needs shared assignment,
internal notes, SLAs, segments, and outbound campaigns, that's what you're
paying for. PocketPing is **just the chat widget and the routing to a
messaging app**. It is consciously not a helpdesk.

### Where operators read and reply

Crisp has its own polished web and mobile apps for operators, which is
genuinely good if a dedicated chat app suits your workflow. PocketPing
routes conversations to **Telegram, Discord, or Slack** — apps your team
already lives in. In Telegram each visitor becomes a **forum topic** so the
inbox stays organized without leaving the app.

### Pricing model

Crisp's free tier is generous for evaluating, but real use crosses into
paid (2-seat limit on free, then per-seat pricing on Pro and above). For a
small team that simply needs reliable chat-on-your-site without a per-seat
clock running, that math gets uncomfortable. PocketPing has no per-seat
cost because there's no "seat" — anyone in your Telegram/Discord/Slack can
reply.

### Self-hosting and data

Crisp is SaaS-only; chats live on Crisp. PocketPing self-hosts behind the
same widget API in three shapes — **serverless lite** (free Cloudflare
Worker), **bridge-server** (Go binary you run), or your **own backend** via
the [SDKs](./sdk) (Node, Python, Go, PHP, Ruby).

## When Crisp is the better choice

You're past one or two operators, you need a real shared inbox with
internal notes and assignment, you want co-browse and a CRM around the
chat, and you're happy paying as your team scales. Crisp is mature and
polished, and replacing it with a chat widget alone would be a downgrade.

## When PocketPing is the better choice

You want a lean chat widget — not a CRM — replies on your phone via
Telegram or Discord or Slack, no per-seat pricing, MIT source you can fork,
and the option to deploy **serverless** with no infrastructure. PocketPing
is a tool for indie hackers, small teams, and self-hosters; Crisp is a
product for sales/CS organizations.
